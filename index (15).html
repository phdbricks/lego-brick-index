Methodology

How the index is calculated

Each January, we record the official retail price from each country's LEGO store (lego.com/{locale}), then convert to USD using the spot exchange rate on January 1st.

Formula

dollar_price = local_price ÷ exchange_rate
composite = mean(all 7 set dollar prices)
index = ((composite ÷ composite_USA) − 1) × 100

Data sources

Official LEGO store (lego.com): used for all countries where LEGO sells directly online and lists prices.
China (yuan / 人民币): prices taken from the LEGO Official Flagship Store on JD.com (乐高官方旗舰店, mall.jd.com/index-790139.html), using list prices in CNY with no discounts applied. The LEGO Group operates this store directly. The lego.com/zh-cn site exists but these sets show no purchasable price there. The CNY (yuan) is used as-is, same as any other currency in the index.

⚠️ No-data countries — Argentina, Chile, Peru, Colombia, and Venezuela appear in the index but have no price data. Argentina has a lego.com/es-ar store, but tracked sets are listed as out of stock with no price. Chile, Peru, Colombia, and Venezuela have no official LEGO online store; sets reach consumers via informal imports or independent retailers whose prices are not comparable.

Caveats

Local VAT is baked into official prices and not removed — the index reflects real consumer cost, not pure currency valuation. LEGO also applies strategic pricing that doesn't track exchange rates precisely.
